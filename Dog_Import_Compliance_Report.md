## 4. Required Documentation

### 4.1 EU Pet Passport
Must contain:
- Animal identification (microchip number)
- Owner details
- Veterinary validation of rabies vaccination
- Date of vaccination: January 13, 2026
- Vaccine batch number and veterinarian signature

### 4.2 Microchip
- Must comply with ISO 11784/11785 standards
- Must be implanted **before** rabies vaccination
- Number must be recorded in pet passport

### 4.3 Owner Declaration

For young animals, Switzerland requires the **signed declaration of non-contact with rabies-susceptible wild animals**. The EU has a standard declaration format and wording; FSVO/BLV links to it.

The declaration template linked by FSVO/BLV includes wording that the signer is either:
- the **owner**, or
- a **natural person who has authorisation in writing from the owner** to carry out the non-commercial movement.

**Official declaration template (PDF):**  
`https://www.blv.admin.ch/dam/blv/en/dokumente/import-export/import/eu-erklaerung-welpen-db.pdf.download.pdf/EU_Erkl%C3%A4rungBesitzer_Welpen_EN.pdf`

**Practical instruction:** Print and complete that PDF using the puppyâ€™s:
- microchip number
- EU pet passport number
- place/date/signature

### 4.4 Written Authorisation for the Family Member (Recommended / Practically Necessary)
Because the FSVO/BLV-linked declaration template explicitly references an **authorised person**, you should prepare a short written authorisation letter that states the family member is authorised to:
- travel with the puppy,
- sign/hold the required declaration on your behalf, and
- present documents to airline staff and border/customs officials.

Keep it with photocopies of your passport/ID and the purchase/ownership documentation dated **December 21, 2025**.
